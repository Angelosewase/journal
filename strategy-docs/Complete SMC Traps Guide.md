## The Shift: Why Traditional SMC is Failing
Traditional SMC traders often wait for a "Change of Character" (CHoCH) or a "Break of Structure" (BOS) and then enter at the nearest Order Block (OB). However, Waqar argues that these "obvious" OBs are often engineered traps.

### 1. The Engineering of Order Blocks
*   Institutions create a "clean" OB to induce SMC traders into a position.
*   The market then "sweeps" this OB to reach a deeper, hidden pool of liquidity.

### 2. The CHoCH Trap
*   A minor shift in structure (CHoCH) on the 1m or 5m timeframe is often just a pullback for a larger HTF move.
*   SMC traders enter at the CHoCH, only to be stopped out as price continues its original HTF trend.

## The SMC Traps Guide: Identifying the 3 Major Traps

### Trap 1: The "SMT" (Smart Money Trap) OB
*   **Description**: A very "clean" and "perfect" looking Order Block that everyone can see.
*   **The Trap**: This OB is often used as **inducement**. Price will tap it briefly, give a small reaction, and then blast through it.
*   **The Solution**: Look for the "extreme" OB or the one that actually swept liquidity *before* it was created.

### Trap 2: Trendline Liquidity (TLL)
*   **Description**: Price creates a series of higher lows or lower highs that form a perfect diagonal.
*   **The Trap**: SMC traders see this as a "strong trend." In reality, it is a massive pool of stop losses.
*   **The Solution**: Wait for the trendline to be **run** (swept) before looking for an entry.

### Trap 3: Equal Highs and Lows (EQH/EQL)
*   **Description**: Double tops or double bottoms.
*   **The Trap**: SMC traders call these "strong resistance" or "strong support." Institutions call them "buy-side" and "sell-side" liquidity.
*   **The Solution**: Never trade *at* EQH/EQL. Always wait for them to be **taken out**.

## The New SMC Framework: Causation vs. Correlation
Waqar argues that traders must focus on **Causation** (why price is moving) rather than **Correlation** (what pattern is forming).

### 1. Market Causation
*   Price moves because it *needs* to find liquidity to fill institutional orders.
*   The "cause" of a move is the sweep of a trap.

### 2. The Inducement Rule
*   A POI is only valid if it has **inducement** before it.
*   If there is no trap for other traders *above* a demand zone (or *below* a supply zone), that zone is likely to fail.

## The Strategy: Trading the "Sweep of the Trap"
1.  **Identify the SMC Trap**: Find the "obvious" OB or CHoCH that most traders are entering at.
2.  **Wait for the Sweep**: Let the market take out those traders' stop losses.
3.  **Enter at the "Extreme"**: Look for the unmitigated POI that sits *behind* the trap.
4.  **Confirm with LTC**: Use a 1m or 5m entry *after* the trap has been cleared.
