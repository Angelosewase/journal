Waqar shares a scalping strategy that he uses to generate $100k/mo in profits. He emphasizes that you don't need a complicated, multi-indicator system. ## The Strategy: The "Underrated" Scalp
This strategy is based on a specific type of **Price Action** and **Volume** interaction. It targets quick "momentum bursts" in the market.

### 1. The Setup: The "Expansion & Retracement"
*   **The Expansion**: Look for a strong, high-volume "expansion" candle that breaks out of a consolidation or a minor structural high/low.
*   **The Retracement**: Wait for a "lazy" or "low-volume" pullback into the 50% level (equilibrium) of that expansion candle.

### 2. The Time Factor (The "Session Filter")
*   This strategy is most effective during the **first 90 minutes** of the London or New York Open.
*   The "opening drive" provides the necessary volatility for the expansion to occur.

## Step-by-Step Execution

### Step 1: Identify the "Opening Range"
*   Observe the price action in the first 15-30 minutes of the session.
*   Mark the high and low of this range.

### Step 2: The "Fakeout" (Liquidity Sweep)
*   Look for a brief move that "fakes out" traders by breaking the range high or low and then immediately reversing.
*   This is the **inducement**—trapping breakout traders.

### Step 3: The "Impulsive Break"
*   After the fakeout, wait for a strong, impulsive candle that breaks the *opposite* side of the opening range.
*   This candle must have a large "body" and small "wicks."

### Step 4: The Entry (The "50% Rule")
*   Place a limit order at the **50% (midpoint)** of the impulsive candle.
*   **Why?** This level often acts as a "magnetic" point where institutions fill remaining orders before the real move continues.

## Risk Management for $100k/mo Scaling
*   **Risk per Trade**: 0.25% to 0.5%. When trading large accounts (e.g., $500k or $1M), small percentages lead to massive dollar profits.
*   **Stop Loss (SL)**: Placed at the "origin" of the impulsive candle.
*   **Take Profit (TP)**: 
    *   **TP1**: 1:2 RR (Take off 50% of the position and move SL to breakeven).
    *   **TP2**: Next major structural level (for 1:3 or 1:5 RR).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
*   **Chasing the Move**: Never enter *after* the price has already moved significantly. Wait for the 50% retracement.
*   **Trading "Mid-Session"**: Volatility often dies down after the first 2 hours. Stick to the opening drive.
*   **Ignoring the Trend**: The expansion candle should ideally align with the HTF (15m or 1h) trend.

## The Mindset of a $100k/mo Trader
*   **Consistency over Home Runs**: Focus on making $5k-$10k per trade consistently rather than trying to "hit it big" on every setup.
*   **Capital Preservation**: The goal is to stay in the game. Protect your capital at all costs.
*   **Discipline**: Having the discipline to *not* trade when the setup isn't there is as important as the trade itself.

## Conclusion
This strategy is "simple" because it focuses on the core mechanics of the market: **Liquidity, Volume, and Time**. By mastering the "Expansion & Retracement" setup during the opening drive, traders can build a high-income scalping business without the need for complex analysis.
