## 1. WEEKLY PREPARATION CHECKLIST

> **Run this every Sunday before markets open (New York time)**

### 🔲 PRE-WEEK SETUP (Sunday Evening)

- [ ] Open **Forex Factory**, **Investing.com**, and **DailyFX** economic calendars
- [ ] Filter events by **High Impact** (red) and **Medium Impact** (orange)
- [ ] Note all central bank speeches scheduled for the week
- [ ] Check **CME FedWatch Tool** for updated rate hike/cut probabilities
- [ ] Review prior week's closed positions and lessons learned
- [ ] Mark key technical levels (S/R, weekly highs/lows) on all pairs you trade
- [ ] Check the **COT (Commitment of Traders)** report released the previous Friday
- [ ] Review the **U.S. Dollar Index (DXY)** weekly chart for trend and structure
- [ ] Check if any G7/G20 meetings or central bank policy meetings are scheduled
- [ ] Verify if any country is in a blackout period (central bank pre-meeting silence)

## 2. DAILY ECONOMIC CALENDAR BREAKDOWN

### ▶ MONDAY

**Typical Market Character:** Thin liquidity early, direction-setting by London open

| Time (EST) | Event | Currency | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| 08:00–09:00 | Flash PMI revisions (if any) | EUR/GBP | Corrects or confirms prior estimates |
| All Day | Weekend geopolitical news digest | All | Risk-on/off tone setter |
| Varies | Speeches from ECB/Fed governors | EUR/USD | Forward guidance signals |

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Check if weekend news created gaps at open
- [ ] Identify Asian session direction (JPY, AUD, NZD momentum)
- [ ] Review if any tier-1 data dropped over the weekend (Japan CPI, China data)
- [ ] Note gap fills — strong gaps often close by Tuesday

---

### ▶ TUESDAY

**Typical Market Character:** Full institutional participation resumes

| Time (EST) | Event | Currency | Importance |
|---|---|---|---|
| 02:00–04:00 | RBA Meeting / Statement (if scheduled) | AUD | Rate decision + tone |
| 04:30 | UK Employment / Wages | GBP | Labour market = BoE pressure |
| 08:30 | U.S. CPI (monthly, 2nd or 3rd Tue) | USD | Most important U.S. inflation gauge |
| 08:30 | U.S. PPI (follows CPI week) | USD | Producer-level inflation signals |
| 10:00 | U.S. JOLTS (Job Openings) | USD | Labor demand indicator |
| Varies | Fed member speeches | USD | Watch for policy hints |

**What to Watch:**
- If CPI releases Tuesday, expect **major volatility on USD pairs**
- A **hot CPI** = USD bullish, gold bearish, stocks bearish
- A **cool CPI** = USD bearish, gold bullish, risk assets rally
- Check if CPI beats/misses Core vs. Headline separately

---

### ▶ WEDNESDAY

**Typical Market Character:** Peak mid-week volatility — highest volume day

| Time (EST) | Event | Currency | Importance |
|---|---|---|---|
| 03:00 | Chinese Trade Balance / CPI | CNY/AUD | Commodity and EM proxy |
| 08:30 | U.S. Core CPI (if not Tue) | USD | Critical inflation reading |
| 10:00 | U.S. ISM Services PMI | USD | Services sector = 70% of U.S. economy |
| 10:30 | U.S. EIA Crude Inventories | CAD/USD | Oil supply = CAD driver |
| 14:00 | FOMC Rate Decision (if scheduled) | USD | **Highest impact event of the month** |
| 14:30 | Fed Press Conference | USD | Chair's tone moves markets |
| Varies | BoC Rate Decision (if scheduled) | CAD | Tier-1 for CAD |

**FOMC Meeting Day Protocol:**
- [ ] Avoid opening new trades 2 hours before the decision
- [ ] Read the full FOMC Statement word-for-word — compare to prior statement
- [ ] Key language to watch: "data-dependent," "restrictive," "appropriate," "vigilant"
- [ ] The **dot plot** (quarterly) shows rate projections — extremely market-moving
- [ ] Watch press conference for Q&A — Chair's tone is often more important than the statement
- [ ] Initial reaction vs. 30-minute reaction often diverge — wait for dust to settle

**BoE / ECB Decision Day Protocol:**
- [ ] Compare current rate to market expectation (priced in vs. surprise)
- [ ] Read Monetary Policy Summary
- [ ] Note **vote split** (e.g., 7-2 for hold vs. 9-0 = very different signals)
- [ ] Watch press conference live — hawkish surprises = currency spike

---

### ▶ FRIDAY

**Typical Market Character:** U.S. session dominates; position squaring before weekend

| Time (EST) | Event | Currency | Importance |
|---|---|---|---|
| 08:30 | **U.S. NFP** (first Friday of month) | USD | **Single most important monthly release** |
| 08:30 | U.S. Unemployment Rate | USD | With NFP — dual mandate check |
| 08:30 | Average Hourly Earnings | USD | Wage inflation = key Fed input |
| 08:30 | Canadian Employment | CAD | Released same time as NFP |
| 10:00 | U.S. Michigan Consumer Sentiment | USD | Forward-looking consumer mood |
| 10:00 | U.S. Existing Home Sales | USD | Housing = leading economic indicator |
| 15:30 | CFTC COT Report (released) | All | Institutional positioning data |

**NFP Day Protocol:**
- [ ] Do NOT trade 15 minutes before NFP
- [ ] Wait for the **initial spike + reversal** (often within 2–5 min)
- [ ] Check: Headline jobs + Prior month revision + Unemployment Rate + Wages
- [ ] A strong NFP + rising wages = Fed stays hawkish = USD bullish
- [ ] Weak NFP + falling wages = rate cut expectations rise = USD bearish
- [ ] Fade extreme moves if fundamentals are mixed

## 3. CENTRAL BANKS & FED WATCH

### 🏛️ U.S. FEDERAL RESERVE (Fed)

**Meeting Schedule:** 8 times per year (approximately every 6 weeks)
**Decisions Released:** 2:00 PM EST
**Press Conference:** 2:30 PM EST (after every meeting)

#### Key Tools & Documents:
| Document | Frequency | What to Look For |
|---|---|---|
| FOMC Statement | Every meeting | Language changes word-by-word |
| Dot Plot | Quarterly (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec) | Rate trajectory projections |
| Beige Book | 2 weeks before each meeting | Regional economic anecdotes |
| Fed Minutes | 3 weeks after each meeting | Internal debate and dissent |
| SEP (Summary of Economic Projections) | Quarterly | GDP, inflation, unemployment forecasts |
| Jackson Hole Speech | Late August | Sets annual policy tone |

#### Fed Blackout Period:
- Starts: **10 days before each FOMC meeting**
- Ends: After the decision and press conference
- During blackout: No Fed speeches — market fills the void with speculation

#### Fed Language Decoder:
| Phrase | What It Means for Traders |
|---|---|
| "Data-dependent" | No pre-committed path — watch each data release |
| "Higher for longer" | Rates staying elevated = USD bullish |
| "Appropriate to hold" | Pause in cycle — neutral/slightly dovish |
| "Vigilant on inflation" | Hawkish — rate hike not off the table |
| "Labor market remains resilient" | No rush to cut |
| "Progress toward our goals" | Moving toward cuts eventually |
| "Risks are two-sided" | Balanced — pivot language emerging |

#### CME FedWatch Tool — Weekly Check:
- URL: **cmegroup.com/markets/interest-rates/cme-fedwatch-tool**
- Look at probability of: **Hold / 25bps cut / 50bps cut** for next 3 meetings
- If market pricing shifts by 10%+ in a week → major catalyst occurred
- Divergence between Fed talk and market pricing = **trading opportunity**

---

### 🏛️ EUROPEAN CENTRAL BANK (ECB)

**Meeting Schedule:** 8 times per year
**Key Figures:** President Christine Lagarde, Chief Economist Philip Lane

#### Watch List:
- Eurozone CPI (Flash Estimate) — released ~last day of each month
- German CPI — leads Eurozone CPI by 1–2 days
- ECB Survey of Professional Forecasters (quarterly)
- ECB Economic Bulletin (bi-weekly)
- Lagarde speeches at conferences (Davos, ECB Forums)
- German Bund yields vs. U.S. Treasury spread = EUR/USD directional clue

---

### 🏛️ BANK OF ENGLAND (BoE)

**Meeting Schedule:** 8 times per year
**Key Figures:** Governor Andrew Bailey, Deputy Governor Dave Ramsden

#### Watch List:
- UK CPI (monthly, released ~2 weeks before BoE meeting)
- UK Average Weekly Earnings (wages are critical for BoE)
- BoE Monetary Policy Report (quarterly) — includes growth/inflation forecasts
- **Vote split** is critical: 5-4 or 6-3 splits signal internal division
- UK GDP (monthly and quarterly)

---

### 🏛️ BANK OF JAPAN (BoJ)

**Meeting Schedule:** 8 times per year
**Key Figures:** Governor Kazuo Ueda

#### Watch List:
- Japan CPI (particularly Core-Core, ex-food and energy)
- **YCC (Yield Curve Control)** adjustments — any tweak causes massive JPY moves
- Japan Tankan Survey (quarterly) — business sentiment
- BoJ Summary of Opinions (released ~1 week after each meeting)
- USD/JPY is ultra-sensitive to U.S. 10yr yield vs. Japan 10yr yield spread
- Government / MoF officials statements on yen weakness/strength

---

### 🏛️ RESERVE BANK OF AUSTRALIA (RBA)

**Meeting Schedule:** 11 times per year (no January meeting)
**Key Figures:** Governor Michele Bullock

#### Watch List:
- Australia CPI (quarterly — March, June, Sep, Dec)
- Monthly CPI Indicator (released monthly)
- Australian Employment / Unemployment Rate
- China economic data (iron ore, trade) — Australia's largest trading partner
- NAB Business Confidence
- RBA Statement on Monetary Policy (quarterly)

---

### 🏛️ BANK OF CANADA (BoC)

**Meeting Schedule:** 8 times per year
**Key Figures:** Governor Tiff Macklem

#### Watch List:
- Canadian CPI (monthly)
- Canada Employment Report (released same time as U.S. NFP)
- Oil prices (WTI/Brent) — Canada is a major oil exporter, CAD correlates strongly
- BoC Business Outlook Survey (quarterly)
- Canada GDP (monthly and quarterly)

---

### 🏛️ SWISS NATIONAL BANK (SNB)

**Meeting Schedule:** Quarterly (March, June, September, December)
**Key Figures:** Chairman Martin Schlegel

#### Watch List:
- Switzerland CPI (very low — SNB targets near 0-2%)
- EUR/CHF — SNB historically defends franc levels
- CHF is a **safe-haven** — surges on global risk-off events
- SNB can intervene in currency markets — watch for official commentary

## 4. KEY ECONOMIC REPORTS & HOW TO TRADE THEM

### 📌 CPI (Consumer Price Index) — Monthly

**Release Time:** 08:30 EST (U.S.)
**Frequency:** Monthly (~2nd or 3rd week)
**Revisions:** Prior month revised

| Reading | USD Impact | Pairs to Watch |
|---|---|---|
| CPI > Forecast (Hot) | USD Bullish | EUR/USD ↓, GBP/USD ↓, Gold ↓ |
| CPI < Forecast (Cool) | USD Bearish | EUR/USD ↑, GBP/USD ↑, Gold ↑ |
| CPI in-line | Muted reaction | Watch other components |

**Checklist for CPI Release:**
- [ ] Headline YoY vs. forecast
- [ ] Core CPI YoY (ex-food & energy) — Fed's preferred measure
- [ ] MoM change vs. prior
- [ ] Shelter / Housing component (sticky, lags rental market)
- [ ] Services inflation (wage-driven, most persistent)
- [ ] Energy component (volatile, can distort headline)

---

### 📌 NFP (Non-Farm Payrolls) — Monthly

**Release Time:** 08:30 EST (First Friday of each month)
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Four-Part Analysis:**
1. **Headline Jobs Added** — How many vs. expected?
2. **Prior Month Revision** — Was last month revised up or down?
3. **Unemployment Rate** — U-3 (headline) and watch U-6 (broader)
4. **Average Hourly Earnings** — Wage inflation = key Fed input

| Scenario | USD Impact |
|---|---|
| Strong jobs + rising wages | Very Bullish USD |
| Strong jobs + flat wages | Moderately Bullish |
| Weak jobs + falling wages | Very Bearish USD |
| Mixed (strong jobs + weak wages) | Uncertain — watch DXY reaction |

**Sectors to Break Down:**
- Government vs. Private sector hiring
- Goods-producing vs. Services-producing
- Full-time vs. Part-time employment (from Household Survey)

---

### 📌 GDP (Gross Domestic Product)

**Release Time:** 08:30 EST
**Frequency:** 
- Advance Estimate: ~4 weeks after quarter end
- Second Estimate: ~8 weeks after quarter end
- Final Estimate: ~12 weeks after quarter end

**Key Components:**
- Personal Consumption (70% of U.S. GDP — most important)
- Business Investment / CapEx
- Government Spending
- Net Exports (Trade Balance contribution)

**Recession Rule of Thumb:** Two consecutive quarters of negative GDP growth

---

### 📌 PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures) — Fed's Preferred

**Release Time:** 08:30 EST
**Frequency:** Monthly (~last week of month)

- **Core PCE Deflator** = Fed's PRIMARY inflation target (2% target)
- More comprehensive than CPI — covers all consumer spending
- Lower weight on housing/shelter than CPI
- A Core PCE above 2.5% = Fed stays hawkish

---

### 📌 PMI (Purchasing Managers Index)

**Types:**
- **Flash PMI** (Preliminary) — Higher market impact
- **Final PMI** — Usually confirms flash
- **ISM** (U.S.) — Different methodology, equally watched

**Key Threshold:** 50.0
- Above 50 = Expansion (bullish for currency)
- Below 50 = Contraction (bearish for currency)
- Rate of change matters — trending up vs. trending down

| PMI Report | Currency | Release Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Global Flash PMIs | EUR, GBP, USD, JPY | ~22nd of each month |
| ISM Manufacturing | USD | First business day of month |
| ISM Services | USD | ~3rd business day of month |
| UK PMIs | GBP | ~First week of month |
| Eurozone PMIs | EUR | ~First week of month |
| China Caixin PMI | CNY/AUD | ~1st of month |

---

### 📌 Retail Sales

**U.S. Release:** 08:30 EST, ~mid-month
**Measures:** Consumer spending at retail level (goods only, not services)
**Revisions:** Prior month often revised — check carefully

- **Core Retail Sales** (ex-autos) — More stable, better trend indicator
- **Control Group** (ex-autos, gas, food, building) — Direct GDP input

---

### 📌 Trade Balance

**USD Impact:** 
- Narrowing deficit = mild USD positive
- Widening deficit = mild USD negative
- More important for commodity currencies (AUD, CAD, NZD)

---

### 📌 JOLTS (Job Openings and Labor Turnover Survey)

**Release:** ~2nd week of month, 10:00 EST
**Key Metrics:**
- Job Openings level (demand for labor)
- **Quits Rate** — Workers quitting = confident in finding new jobs = tight labor market
- Hires vs. Separations

---

### 📌 Initial Jobless Claims (Weekly)

**Release:** Every Thursday, 08:30 EST
**What It Shows:** New unemployment insurance filings
- **Below 220K** = Strong labor market
- **Above 260K** = Labor market loosening
- Weekly data — watch 4-week moving average for trend

## 5. MARKET SENTIMENT ANALYSIS

### 📊 COT Report (Commitment of Traders)

**Released:** Every Friday at 15:30 EST (covers data through Tuesday)
**Source:** CFTC — cftc.gov/reports/commitmentoftraders

**Three Trader Categories:**
1. **Commercials** (Hedgers) — Largest players, often contrarian signal
2. **Non-Commercials** (Large Speculators) — Trend followers — follow these
3. **Non-Reportables** (Small Speculators) — Retail traders

**How to Read for Forex:**
- Track **Net Non-Commercial positions** for each major currency
- Large net longs = bullish speculative positioning
- Extreme net longs/shorts = potential for **position squeeze reversal**
- Shift in direction (flipping from net short to net long) = trend change signal

**Currencies to Track Weekly:**
- EUR (Euro FX futures)
- GBP (British Pound futures)
- JPY (Japanese Yen futures)
- CHF (Swiss Franc futures)
- CAD (Canadian Dollar futures)
- AUD (Australian Dollar futures)
- NZD (New Zealand Dollar futures)

---

### 📊 DXY (U.S. Dollar Index) — Weekly Bias

The DXY measures USD against a basket: EUR (57.6%), JPY (13.6%), GBP (11.9%), CAD (9.1%), SEK (4.2%), CHF (3.6%)

**Weekly DXY Analysis:**
- [ ] Identify weekly trend (higher highs/lows vs. lower highs/lows)
- [ ] Key weekly S/R levels
- [ ] RSI divergence (daily/weekly)
- [ ] Comparison to U.S. 10-year Treasury yield (usually correlated)
- [ ] DXY rising = headwind for EUR/USD, GBP/USD, commodities

---

### 📊 Risk Sentiment Gauge

**Risk-ON Indicators (sell USD, JPY, CHF / buy AUD, NZD, EM):**
- S&P 500 and Nasdaq rising
- VIX below 15 (low fear)
- Gold flat or falling
- Bitcoin and crypto rising
- EM (emerging market) currencies gaining

**Risk-OFF Indicators (buy USD, JPY, CHF / sell AUD, NZD):**
- S&P 500 falling
- VIX above 20–25 (elevated fear)
- Gold surging
- Bond yields falling (flight to safety)
- Credit spreads widening

**Track Daily:**
- [ ] S&P 500 e-mini futures direction (pre-market signal)
- [ ] VIX level (options fear gauge)
- [ ] U.S. 10-Year Treasury Yield (key USD driver)
- [ ] Gold spot price direction
- [ ] Crude oil (CAD correlation)

## ⚠️ GOLDEN RULES FOR FUNDAMENTAL TRADING

1. **The market prices in expectations, not reality** — a "good" number that misses expectations can tank a currency
2. **Reaction to news matters more than the news itself** — if USD doesn't rally on hot CPI, it's a warning
3. **Rate differentials are the primary driver of FX** — always know where yields are heading
4. **Don't fight the Fed** — central bank direction trumps most other fundamentals
5. **Fundamentals set direction; technicals set entry** — combine both
6. **Never hold through tier-1 events unless you understand the risk** — NFP and FOMC can move 100+ pips in seconds
7. **Macro takes time to play out** — a quarterly trend is more reliable than a daily narrative
8. **High impact data can invalidate your entire weekly bias** — stay flexible
9. **Liquidity matters** — fundamentals work better during London/NY overlap (1:00–5:00 PM GMT)
10. **Position size for events** — reduce size before known high-risk releases
