## The Liquidity Cycle: How Markets Move
Waqar explains that the market moves in a continuous cycle of **Liquidity Generation** and **Liquidity Consumption**.

### 1. Liquidity Generation (The "Setup")
*   Institutions create "patterns" (Double Tops, Trendlines, Support/Resistance) that retail traders use to place their stop losses.
*   This "generation" phase creates a pool of buy-side and sell-side liquidity.

### 2. Liquidity Consumption (The "Sweep")
*   Once enough liquidity is generated, institutions "run" (sweep) those levels to fill their large orders.
*   The sweep is often an aggressive, fast move that "clears the board" of retail stops.

## The 3 Major Types of Liquidity (The "Triple Threat")

### Type 1: Buy-Side & Sell-Side Liquidity (BSL/SSL)
*   **BSL**: Located above structural highs (Equal Highs, Swing Highs).
*   **SSL**: Located below structural lows (Equal Lows, Swing Lows).
*   **Institutional Goal**: To "take out" BSL/SSL before moving in the opposite direction.

### Type 2: Trendline Liquidity (TLL)
*   Liquidity that builds up along a diagonal line.
*   Retail traders buy/sell at the "third touch" of a trendline.
*   Institutions wait for the trendline to be **broken** (swept) before the real move occurs.

### Type 3: Internal & External Liquidity
*   **External Liquidity**: Major swing highs and lows on the HTF (Daily, 4h).
*   **Internal Liquidity**: Minor structural points on the LTF (15m, 5m, 1m).
*   **The Rule**: Price moves from **Internal Liquidity** to **External Liquidity** (and vice-versa).

## The 15-Minute Masterclass: Step-by-Step Liquidity Analysis

### Step 1: Mark the "Liquidity Pools"
*   Identify the most "obvious" levels of support/resistance, trendlines, and equal highs/lows on your chart.
*   These are the pools that institutions will target.

### Step 2: Identify the "Inducement" (The Trap)
*   Look for the minor structural break (CHoCH) or Order Block that sits *just before* a major liquidity pool.
*   This is the **inducement**—the final trap for retail traders before the real move.

### Step 3: Wait for the "Grab & Go"
*   Wait for price to aggressively sweep the liquidity pool and the inducement.
*   Look for a strong rejection (a "wick" or a large reversal candle) immediately after the sweep.

### Step 4: The Entry (The "LTC")
*   Drop down to a lower timeframe (1m or 5m) *after* the sweep.
*   Wait for a clean break of structure (BOS) and enter at the retracement to a valid POI.
